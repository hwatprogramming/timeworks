The Idea

Before starting, I have decided that this software should be useable from both mobile and PC and have settled on web to build my program.

I have also thought about offline usage but I am not experienced enough to know what framework to use for my program.

The program is spilt up into 5 parts: Input, Generate, Scan, Score, Sort/Filter, Export

Part 1: Getting Information

The required information to generate a schedule are: shift details, worker details, requirements, limitations and preferences

A form will be used to gather required information from the user

I have thought about 2 methods for acquiring information(User Interface): Sentence Forming and/or Graphical Input

User will be prompted with an option to create a new schedule or load an existing .json settings file

If user selects new, a default .json file will be parse through and stored in Javascript

If user selects load, Javascript will verifiy if it is a valid file then if valis, the .json file will be parse through and stored in Javascript

    • Part 3: Scanning and scoring the combination

    There are 2 components to this: scanning and sorting

    The scanning portion will make use of a combing system that looks for certain patterns

    The scoring portion will detect a certain pattern and assign points accordingly

    The scoring will also use standard deviation and variance to generate a balanced score across the workers for some factors.

    There are 5 factors that I have thought of to score every schedule combination by:

  • Rest Score: Amount and quality of rest
  • Shift Change/Pattern Score: Worker's preference for certain shift patterns
  • Shift Score: Worker's preference for certain shift
  • Relaibility Score: How often a reliable senior is available
  • Replaceability Score: How easy it is to replace someone on a particular day

    • These are the 5 factors explained in the way that I imagined it in my head.

  • Shift Score: Worker's preference for certain shift
    • detects shifts that workers like and gives 1 point
  • Shift Change/Pattern Score: Worker's preference for certain shift patterns
    • detects shift patterns that workers like and gives 1 point
    • detects shift patterns that workers hate and deduct 1 point
    • detects improbable shift patterns that workers and discards the combination(i should program this into the generator instead)
  • Rest Score: Amount and quality of rest
    • For monthly(per day) Schedule
      • Balanced Profile: Incentivize 2-3 day breaks
        • detects 1-day rest and gives 1 point
        • detects 2-day rest and gives 3 point
        • detects 3-day rest and gives 6 point
      • Choppy Profile: Incentivize 1 day breaks
        • detects 1-day rest and gives 2 point
        • detects 2-day rest and gives 3 point
        • detects 3-day rest and gives 5 point
      • Grind Hard Rest Hard Profile: Incentivize 1 day breaks and more than 3 day breaks
        • detects 1-day rest and gives 2 point
        • detects 2-day rest and gives 2 point
        • detects 3-day rest and gives 3 point
        • detects more than 3-day rest and gives 9 point
    • For daily(hour) Schedule: Shifts are stacked (default) towards night/day, discourages medium duration breaks due to lack of quality sleep (4-8 hour breaks)?
      • Balanced Profile: Incentivize 2-3 day breaks
        • detects 1-3 hour rests and gives 1 point/hour
        • detects 4-8 hour rests and gives 0.5 point/hour(deduct points)
        • detects more than 8 hour rests and gives 1 point/hour
  • Relaibility Score: How often a reliable senior is available
    • detect the amount of days/hour where at least 1 senior is around and gives 1 point
  • Replaceability Score: How easy it is to replace someone on a particular day
    • Incentivize longs breaks and shift patterns where it is easy to slot someone in to replace
    • Imagine a worker working 2 days, resting 3 days, it is easier(more humane) to slot him in front of his 2 day shift because he still has a 2 day break aftewards
    • Imagine a worker working 3 days, resting 2 days, it is harder(more inhumane) to slot him in front of his 3 day shift because he only has a 1 day break aftewards after working 4 days

    Part 4: Sorting and Filtering combinations

    The program will rank the schedule combinations via a default order(Rest to Pattern to Shift to Reliability to Replaceability). The user can also change the order or sorting.

    There will also be a blank schedule to allow user input to find a pattern that matches his input.

    Part 5: Displaying & Exporting

    The final step will be to display the schedule and translate these set of data into an excel, google sheet or image file.
